How to flash it to a USB

You'll need a USB drive with at least 4GB of space. We recommend using Balena Etcher — it's free, simple, and works on Windows, Mac, and Linux.

Once flashed, boot from the USB (usually by pressing F12 or F2 at startup) and follow the on-screen installer.

# Or flash from terminal on Linux/Mac:
sudo dd if=lukilinux-1.0.iso of=/dev/sdX bs=4M status=progress

Verify your download

Always a good idea to verify the ISO before flashing. Compare the SHA256 hash below with the one on your downloaded file.

# On Linux/Mac:
sha256sum lukilinux-1.0.iso

# On Windows (PowerShell):
Get-FileHash lukilinux-1.0.iso -Algorithm SHA256

System requirements

LukiLinux runs on most hardware made in the last 15 years. If it was a capable machine back then, it'll run just fine now.

CPU: Any 64-bit x86 processor
RAM: 1 GB minimum (2 GB recommended)
Storage: 10 GB minimum (HDD supported)
Desktop: XFCE 4.18
Architecture: x86_64
